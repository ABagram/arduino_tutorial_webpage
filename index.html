Procedures

Connecting the Board to the Development Environment

  1. Plug the Arduino Uno board to one of the USB ports of the laptop/ computer through the USB Type-A to USB Type-B cable. This should light up the LED marked "ON" on the Arduino Uno to indicate that the board is now being powered.
  2. Launch the Arduino IDE.
    1. Tools  Board, then select Arduino Uno.
       Warning!
      Laptops/ computers generally can recognize that there is a USB device connected, but are not able to automatically identify it as an Arduino board.
    2. Tools  Port, then select the communication (COM) port that corresponds to the Arduino.

      The port can be identified through the system’s Device Manager, wherein the Arduino Uno appears as an “Unknown device”.

Connecting the Breadboard to the Arduino


Some breadboards do not have markings that indicate which power rails (strips) are positivetypically marked by a red line and a plus  sign, or negativetypically marked by either a blue or black line and a negative  sign.

In such case, simply assign the rails.

Make sure to keep it consistent to prevent accidental short circuits.
Learn more. How to Use a Breadboard
  1. To use both positive railsused to provide the positive voltage to power electronic components in the circuit & connect to the positive terminal of the power source of the breadboard interchangeably, connect them with an M/M jumper wire. Plug one end into a hole on one rail and the other end into a hole on the other rail.
    Tip.
    Use a red jumper wire to indicate positive power supply connection for consistency.
  2. Connect one of the holes within the power rail to the 5V pin of the Arduino Uno using an M/M jumper wire.
  3. To use both negative railsused to provide the ground reference for the circuit & connect components that require a ground connection of the breadboard interchangeably, connect them with an M/M jumper wire. Plug one end into a hole on one rail and the other end into a hole on the other rail.
